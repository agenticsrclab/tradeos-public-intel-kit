# TradeOS Business Thesis

TradeOS is the crypto-market vertical of a broader source-grounded Data
Intelligence OS strategy. It turns fragmented market, token, source, risk, and
outcome data into reviewable intelligence packets for users, builders, and
agents before decisions or workflows depend on them.

This page is public-safe positioning for builders. It explains what TradeOS is,
what business it is in, who it serves, and how builders can package it without
turning TradeOS into a broker, custodian, hosted execution system, or managed
account service.

## What Business TradeOS Is In

TradeOS is in the business of source-grounded crypto market intelligence.

It supplies:

- token identity and source evidence;
- market state, regime, flow, and risk context;
- public thesis intelligence and material-change follow-up;
- Fusion Signals and other bounded signal-quality views;
- watchlists, dossiers, and Ask TradeOS;
- feedback targets, outcome memory, validation, replay, and proof;
- builder APIs, SDKs, MCP tools, public-intel keys, and x402/API paths;
- paid/private intelligence products when workflows need production depth.

The product is not raw endpoint resale. The value is the intelligence packet:

```text
identity -> evidence -> freshness -> caveats -> confidence -> feedback -> outcome memory
```

Builders package that packet into products customers already understand:
cockpits, bots, briefings, dashboards, reports, plugins, validation packs,
watchlists, and proof pages.

## The Problem

Crypto tools can look polished while still relying on weak context:

| Weak layer | What goes wrong |
| --- | --- |
| Token identity | A symbol-only answer points at the wrong chain, contract, or copycat asset. |
| Freshness | Old evidence is repeated as current. |
| Source evidence | A model answer sounds fluent but cannot be inspected or disputed. |
| Risk context | Liquidity, sellability, uncertainty, and caveats do not travel with the output. |
| Feedback and outcomes | Useful, stale, wrong, early, late, and missing-context labels do not improve the next read. |
| Audit trail | Operators cannot reconstruct why a claim or signal appeared. |

The agent economy makes the gap larger. Agents increasingly summarize, route
workflows, call tools, coordinate with other agents, and hand context to local
systems. A weak intelligence packet can propagate through bots, dashboards,
alerts, APIs, and provider routes before a person notices that the context was
stale or under-sourced.

See [Problem Space](problem-space.md) for the full cost-of-missing-intelligence
framing and magnitude anchors.

## Target Customers

TradeOS can serve:

- curious crypto users who want evidence instead of hype;
- traders and researchers reviewing market context before action;
- token teams monitoring identity, liquidity, narrative, source, and risk
  context;
- paid communities that need source-backed market briefings and follow-up;
- agent and bot builders that need reviewable packets, not raw prose;
- funds, analysts, and quant teams that need validation, replay, proof, and
  outcome memory;
- builders packaging TradeOS intelligence into workflows, dashboards, bots,
  reports, private cockpits, or agent tools.

## Business Model

The public value ladder is:

```text
Use TradeOS free.
Earn Data Intel Credits by improving intelligence quality.
Build and earn on public intelligence.
Pay when you need private intelligence products, scale, alerts, automation, or data rights.
```

TradeOS creates demand by making public intelligence useful before payment. It
earns when a workflow needs:

- deeper history or larger symbol coverage;
- private intelligence products;
- alert delivery, webhooks, API delivery, or x402 resources;
- validation APIs, replay datasets, or proof workflows;
- automation-safe reads;
- enterprise contracts, custom universes, support, or explicit data rights.

Builders can monetize the workflow around TradeOS intelligence. TradeOS remains
the source-grounded intelligence and paid-depth layer.

## Market Opportunity

The older "crypto trading tools and bots" TAM is not the right primary frame
for the current product boundary. It is adjacent, but TradeOS is better framed
across:

- crypto market data and analytics;
- source-grounded research and token-risk intelligence;
- agent-ready intelligence infrastructure;
- validation, proof, replay, and feedback-aware workflow tools;
- builder APIs, x402 resources, and private intelligence products.

Public copy should avoid unsourced numeric TAM claims. A numeric TAM/SAM/SOM
should be published only after a sourced refresh and should clearly separate:

- **TradeOS** as the crypto-market vertical; and
- the broader **Source Intelligence Network** / vertical Data Intelligence OS
  direction.

## TradeOS And Source Intelligence Network

TradeOS is one vertical. The broader company direction is a network of
source-grounded vertical Data Intelligence OS products.

```text
TradeOS = crypto market Data Intelligence OS vertical
Source Intelligence Network = broader cross-vertical direction
```

Public builder docs can describe this relationship at a high level. They should
not publish patent-sensitive mechanics such as exact quality-ledger flows,
source-weighting algorithms, credit-allocation formulas, abuse-control
internals, cross-vertical ledger mechanics, private routes, thresholds, schemas,
or deployment runbooks.

## Boundary

TradeOS public intelligence is research and review context. It does not custody
assets, hold exchange credentials, manage accounts, place orders, provide
personalized financial advice, or guarantee outcomes.

Private self-hosted cockpit products may turn evidence into local
recommendations, but final decisions, account controls, custody, sizing,
approvals, and execution remain with the self-hosted operator or a separate
product outside TradeOS public intelligence.
